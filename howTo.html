Garfenomics

How to play

You play the game by clicking on the garfcoin in the center to generate some initial currency. From there you can purchase upgrades from the right side of the screen which influence your passive income. Starting at various times, you will have a chance of getting hacked, to prevent these hacks you must purchase security upgrades from the left column. These upgrades are unlocked by purchasing security experts. When a hack pops up at the bottom of the screen, press the red X button to make it disappear.

Company Upgrades

Developers: For each developer, you gain one garfcoin per click, and passively gain 1 garfcoin per second. Increases the likelihood of phishing attacks and insider attacks.
Security Experts: Passively generates 5 garfcoin per second. You must purchase security experts in order to purchase security upgrades.
PCs: Double the amount of garfcoin you gain per click (excluding the amount gained from developers) Increases the likelihood of ransomware attacks.
Offices: Passively generates 1,000 garfcoin per second. Increases the likelihood of DDoS attacks
Jim Davis: The creator of Garfield. Passively generates 100,000 garfcoin per second.

Hacks

Phishing Attack: An employee has fallen for a fraudulent email and has revealed company information. Lose a sum of money that increases with each subsequent attack.
Ransomware Attack: An attacker has planted malware on a computer and will not let you access your files until you pay them. All active and passive income is stopped until the required amount is paid. Insider Attack: A developer steals from your company. Lose 1/3 of your money and 1 developer.
DDoS Attack: Your network have been attacked and cannot be used. All passive gain is cancelled for 10 seconds.

Security Upgrades

Purchasing security upgrades increases the likelihood of being successfully attacked. However, increasing the size of your company will increase the likelihood of being attacked. It is important to balance the two upgrade systems.
Employee Training: Teach your employees to recognize possible threats. Reduces the likelihood of phishing attacks.
Antivirus: Install 3rd party antivirus on your computers. Reduces the likelihood of ransomware.
Access Control: Limit what employees can access. Reduces the likelihood of insider attacks.
DDoS Protection: Install 3rd party software to secure company servers and networks. Reduces the likelihood of DDoS attacks.
